California's 2026 Governor's Race

What's at stake in the June 2 primary — and why Democratic voters need to pay attention now.

California's top-two primary system mandates that all candidates appear on one ballot, with the two highest vote-getters advancing to the general election regardless of party. For the June 2, 2026 primary, eight Democrats and two Republicans are competing to replace Governor Gavin Newsom.

Current polling shows Democratic support divided among several candidates, with no single Democrat consistently clearing 15 percent. Republican candidates Steve Hilton and Chad Bianco have consolidated support at 16 percent and 14 percent respectively. Steve Hilton, a former Fox News host, recently secured Donald Trump's official endorsement for his "Califordable" platform. Chad Bianco, the Riverside County Sheriff, remains a vocal Trump supporter despite being passed over for that endorsement in favor of Hilton.

A victory for either Republican would establish a direct alliance between the California governor's office and the Trump administration, reversing years of state-level resistance to federal policies. Statistical models estimate a 27 percent chance that both Republicans finish in the top two. If this occurs, no Democrat will appear on the November ballot. This outcome depends on whether Democratic voters consolidate or if high fragmentation continues through the primary.

Key Dates

  • May 4 — Ballots begin mailing
  • May 5 — Ballot drop-off opens
  • May 18 — Voter registration deadline
  • June 2 — Primary Election Day
