Spread the Word on the Governor's Race

Urgent

California's top-two primary means the two highest vote-getters advance to November — regardless of party. Eight Democrats are currently splitting the vote in the June 2 primary, while Republicans Steve Hilton (now backed by Trump) and Chad Bianco have consolidated at 16 and 14 percent respectively. Statistical models put the odds of both Republicans finishing in the top two at 27 percent. If that happens, no Democrat appears on the November ballot.

The most important thing you can do right now is make sure every Democrat in your life understands what's at stake. Talk to friends, family, and neighbors. Share this information on social media. The primary is June 2 — voter registration deadline is May 18, ballots mail May 4.

What to tell people

"California's governor's race could end up with no Democrat on the November ballot if Democratic voters don't consolidate in the June primary. Eight Democrats are splitting the vote while two Republicans — one with Trump's endorsement — are ahead. Talk to everyone you know about this race before June 2."

Tell Democratic Electeds: Endorse Now

Urgent

Democratic fragmentation in the governor's race is the core problem. One of the most effective ways to consolidate the vote is for prominent Democratic officeholders to endorse a candidate now — before the primary — so voters have a clear signal. Contact your state and local representatives and ask them to make an endorsement.

West Hollywood residents are represented by Assembly Member Rick Chavez Zbur (AD-51), State Senator Ben Allen (SD-26), and members of the LA County Board of Supervisors. Your Congressional representatives matter too.

Sample Message

"I am a constituent writing to urge you to endorse a Democratic candidate in the 2026 California governor's race as soon as possible. With eight Democrats splitting the vote and two Republicans consolidated, there is a real risk that no Democrat advances to November. An early endorsement from you could help consolidate Democratic support and prevent that outcome."
