Protect Immigrant Neighbors

Urgent

West Hollywood has publicly condemned recent federal immigration actions and is actively sharing Know Your Rights resources for immigrant community members. Help defend our neighbors by sharing accurate information, supporting local legal and mutual-aid efforts, and urging city leaders to keep West Hollywood firmly opposed to cooperation with federal immigration enforcement.

Contact your City Council representatives to reinforce West Hollywood's commitment to non-cooperation, share Know Your Rights resources in your networks, and support local organizations providing legal aid and direct assistance to immigrant residents.

Sample Message to City Council

"My name is [YOUR NAME] and I am a West Hollywood resident. I urge the City Council to maintain West Hollywood's strong commitment to protecting all residents, regardless of immigration status, and to remain fully opposed to cooperation with federal immigration enforcement. Please ensure our city continues to be a place of refuge for our immigrant neighbors."

Build Power for the 2026 Elections

Ongoing

The road to the 2026 midterms starts now, with California's June 2 primary as the next key step. Join West Hollywood voter outreach, help register and inform voters, and organize early so we can elect leaders who will defend democracy and vulnerable communities.

California ballots begin mailing May 4, ballot drop-off opens May 5, and the voter registration deadline is May 18. Start conversations with your neighbors now — the earlier we build momentum, the stronger our turnout.
