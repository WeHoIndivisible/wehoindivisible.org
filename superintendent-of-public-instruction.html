WeHo Indivisible · A Voter's Brief · May 8, 2026

The Superintendent Race

Ten candidates. Two reach November. Don't let one of them be Sonja Shaw.

The California Superintendent of Public Instruction oversees public education for nearly six million students. On June 2, voters narrow ten candidates to two, who advance to November regardless of party. Seven Democrats are splitting the vote. One Republican is consolidating it. If she finishes in the top two, the November ballot will feature an avowed culture warrior whose campaign attacks transgender children.

The Threat

Sonja Shaw

School board president, Chino Valley Unified. Endorsed by the California Republican Party, Moms for Liberty, and the California Rifle and Pistol Association.

Girls are losing roster spots, medals and scholarships.

Sonja Shaw, on transgender athletes

Shaw's platform centers on two demands: mandatory parent notification when a child expresses gender-identity issues at school, and the exclusion of transgender students from girls' athletic teams. She has built a public profile as a MAGA culture warrior; her endorsement list reflects that. With seven Democrats splitting the vote, she does not need to win a majority. She needs only to finish second.
